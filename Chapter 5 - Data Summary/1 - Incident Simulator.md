# Incident Simulator & Operational Readiness 🚨
## **Topics Covered**
- Incident investigation across the **data platform stack**
- Debugging failures in pipeline components (storage, compute, orchestration, query layers)
- Reading **logs, metrics, and system signals**
- Root cause analysis and structured troubleshooting
- Writing clear **incident resolution documentation**
- Team-based incident handling and coordination

> ⚠️ **Note:**
> This exercise simulates real operational incidents that may occur in production systems.  
> You must **ask your mentor how to access the Incident Simulator environment** before starting.  
> Each incident must be solved independently and documented.

### ⏳ Timeline
Estimated Duration: **4 Days + 1 Team Incident Day**

- **Day 1–4: Individual Incident Handling**
  - Ask your mentor how to **enter the Incident Simulator environment**.
  - Work through the incidents one by one.
  - For each incident:
    - Investigate the issue
    - Identify the root cause
    - Implement or propose the resolution
  - Write a **short documentation entry** explaining:
    - The symptoms of the incident
    - The investigation process
    - The root cause
    - The final solution
  - Submit your solution and documentation to your mentor for review.

  > If the solution **does not meet the mentor's standard**, the mentor may **recreate the incident**, and you will be required to solve it again.

- **Team Incident Day (Scheduled Separately)**
  - At a later stage, **all new team members will work together** on a larger simulated incident.
  - This incident will involve **multiple technologies across the stack** and will simulate a **real production-style failure scenario**.
  - The exercise will focus on:
    - Team coordination
    - Incident ownership and communication
    - Real-world troubleshooting workflow
    - Handling complex, multi-system failures

  > This team exercise **may not occur immediately after the first four days**, as it requires multiple trainees to reach this stage.  
  > You may need to **wait for other trainees** before participating.

## Overview
The goal of the Incident Simulator is to develop the ability to **diagnose and resolve real-world production issues** across the technologies you previously learned.

Rather than focusing on theory, this exercise emphasizes:
- **Practical troubleshooting**
- Understanding system interactions
- Building a clear and structured **incident investigation process**

The incidents may involve different parts of the stack, including storage, compute engines, orchestration tools, or query systems.

## Wrapping Up :trophy:
By the end of this exercise you should be able to:

- Systematically **investigate production issues**
- Identify and explain **root causes**
- Propose and validate **solutions**
- Document incidents clearly so others can learn from them
- Collaborate with teammates during **multi-system incidents**
